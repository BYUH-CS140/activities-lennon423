Mountain

Gulugod Baboy

"Gulugod baboy" in Filipino translates to "pig's spine" and refers to a specific mountain in Mabini, Batangas, Philippines. It's a popular hiking destination known for its steep terrain, three distinct peaks (including the highest, Pinagbanderahan), and stunning panoramic views of the sea, green hills, and nearby mountains. The hike is considered moderate to challenging, taking around 2 hours to complete.

Beyond its aquatic allure, Mabini is also steeped in history and culture. Visitors can explore historical sites like the San Miguel Arcangel Parish Church, which showcases beautiful architecture and local traditions. The town is also known for its annual festivals, such as the Pista ng Bayan, which celebrates local culture through vibrant parades and traditional performances.
